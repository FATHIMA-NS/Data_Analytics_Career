# Foundations: Data & Analysis

## Introduction & Section 1
### Core Principles

- **Definition of Data:** A collection of facts.
    
- **Definition of Analysis:** The process of drawing conclusions from data.
    
- **The Success Formula:**
    
    $$\text{Job Success} = \text{Computer} + \text{Brain} + \text{Skills} + \text{Traits}$$
    

### The Data Lifecycle (Six Phases)

This is the framework for every data project you will undertake.

|**Phase**|**Objective**|
|---|---|
|**Ask**|Understand the business problem defined by the stakeholder.|
|**Prepare**|Collect, identify, and verify accurate data sources.|
|**Process**|Clean, organize, and format data to ensure consistency.|
|**Analyze**|Perform calculations and identify patterns/trends.|
|**Share**|Visualize and present findings (e.g., Sheets, Tableau).|
|**Act**|Apply insights to implement business strategies.|

### Data Types in Spreadsheets

Understanding these types is critical for selecting the correct formulas and analysis methods.

- **Numerical:** Used for math/measurements (e.g., price, height, age).
    
- **Date:** Represents points in time (e.g., timestamps, shipping dates).
    
- **Text:** Used for identification, not calculation (e.g., names, IDs).
    
- **Categorical:** Represents discrete labels/groups (e.g., product colors).
    

###  Industry Specialization

- **People Analytics (HR/Workforce Analytics):** Using workforce data to gain insights and improve company operations.
    
- **The Data Analyst Persona:** An explorer, a detective, and an artist.
    

### Domains of Data Science

Specialization is necessary because one person cannot know everything. The domain is determined by the goal of your decision-making:

- **Analytics:** Encountering the unknown to understand the world.
    
- **Statistics:** Making a few, important decisions under uncertainty.
    
- **Machine Learning:** Automating many decisions under uncertainty.

## Section 2: Understand The Data Ecosystem

### Data Analysis Process Models

While different models exist, the **core concepts** remain consistent across all frameworks.

|**Process Model**|**Phases / Steps**|
|---|---|
|**Standard Lifecycle**|Ask, Prepare, Process, Analyze, Share, Act|
|**EMC (Dell EMC)**|Discovery, Pre-Processing, Model Planning, Model Building, Communication, Operationalize|
|**SAS (Iterative)**|Ask, Prepare, Explore, Model, Implement, Act, Evaluate|
|**Big Data Process**|Business Case, Identification, Acquisition/Filtering, Extraction, Validation/Cleaning, Aggregation/Representation, Analysis, Visualization, Utilization|

_Note: The **SAS process** is frequently visualized as an infinite symbol to denote its iterative, repeatable nature._

### Definitions & Concepts

- **Data Ecosystem:** A group of elements (hardware, software, and people) that interact to produce, manage, store, organize, analyze, and share data.
    
- **Cloud:** A storage environment for keeping data online rather than on a local hard drive.
    
- **Data Analysis:** The collection, transformation, and organization of data to draw conclusions, make predictions, and drive decisions.
    
- **Data Analytics:** The science of data.
    
- **Data Science vs. Analytics:**
    
    - **Data Scientist:** Finds _new questions_ using data to model the unknown.
        
    - **Data Analyst:** Finds _answers_ for existing questions.
        

### Making Data-Driven Decisions

**Data-Driven Decision Making (DDDM):** Using facts to guide business strategy.

- **The Formula:** `Data + Business Knowledge = Mystery Solved`
    
- **The Role of Intuition:** Data alone can be biased. Intuition should be paired with business knowledge and validated by **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)**.
    
- **Key Project Questions:**
    
    1. What results are needed?
        
    2. Who is the audience?
        
    3. Am I answering the actual question asked?
        
    4. How quickly is a decision required?
        

## Section 3: Embrace Your Data Analyst Skills

### Analytical Skills

Analytical skills are the qualities and characteristics associated with solving problems using facts. The five key skills include:

1. **Curiosity:** The desire to learn and explore.
    
2. **Understanding Context:** Recognizing the "why" behind the data.
    
3. **Technical Mindset:** Breaking down large, complex tasks into smaller, manageable steps.
    
4. **Data Design:** How you organize and structure information.
    
5. **Data Strategy:** Managing the people, processes, and tools involved in analytics.
