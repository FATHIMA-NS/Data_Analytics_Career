# The Wonderful World Of Data

- Data has no life , but analyst bring data to life via spreadsheets , database , query languages , visualization tools etc. 
- Data has a life cycle of its own 

## Section 1 : Follow The Data Life Cycle
### Phases Of Data Analysis

	1. Ask : Define the problem and confirm stakeholder expectations
	2. Prepare : Collect and store data for analysis
	3. Process : Clean and transform data to ensure integrity
	4. Analyze : Use data analysis tools to draw conclusions
	5. Share :Interpret and communicate results to others to make Data
			Driven Decisions
	6. Act : Put your insights to work in order to solve the original problem

### Stages Of The Data Life Cycle

1. **Plan:** Decide what kind of data is needed, how it will be managed, and who will be responsible for it.
    
2. **Capture:** Collect or bring in data from a variety of different sources.
    
3. **Manage:** Care for and maintain the data. This includes determining how and where it is stored and the tools used to do so.
    
4. **Analyze:** Use the data to solve problems, make decisions, and support business goals.
    
5. **Archive:** Keep relevant data stored for long-term and future reference.
    
6. **Destroy:** Remove data from storage and delete any shared copies of the data.

- There are variations is the stages of **Data life Cycle** depending of the Organizations, refer this [[Variations Of The Data Life Cycle]]

### Example Of Data Analysis Process

> [!EXAMPLE] Running Case Study: Employee Engagement Survey
> 
> The speaker uses an **Employee Engagement Survey** to demonstrate how to move from a raw problem to a business solution using the 6-phase framework.

### The 6-Phase Lifecycle

|**Phase**|**Action**|**Engagement Survey Example**|
|---|---|---|
|**1. Ask**|Define the goal|What is the purpose of this survey? What do leaders need to know about team sentiment?|
|**2. Prepare**|Gather resources|Collect both **quantitative** (ratings) and **qualitative** (written feedback) data.|
|**3. Process**|Ensure integrity|Clean the data; check for typos, outliers, and systematic missing responses.|
|**4. Analyze**|Extract patterns|Run the planned analyses; let the data "speak" without letting personal intuition bias the results.|
|**5. Share**|Report findings|Present a high-level "landscape view" to executives so they understand the organization's state.|
|**6. Act**|Drive change|Implement organizational and team-level interventions to improve the employee experience.|

### 3 Golden Rules for Analysts

 Remember these to stay objective:

1. **The "Handshake" Rule:** The **Process** phase is your introduction to the data. Use this time to understand the structure, quirks, and nuances of the information.
    
2. **The "Storyteller" Rule:** You are a storyteller, but it is **not your story**. You are merely the amplifier for the data. If your intuition says one thing, but the data says another, always trust the data.
    
3. **The "Act" Rule:** The analysis is just the middle step. The real value is created during the **Act** phase, where insights are converted into actual interventions.
    

> [!WARNING] The Risk of Skipping Steps
> 
> The data analysis process is rigorous and lengthy. Attempting to skip steps—such as jumping straight from "Ask" to "Analyze"—will result in a failure to elicit the true insights you are looking for.

## Section 3 : The Data Analysis Toolbox

### Tools Data Analyst Uses
1. **Spreadsheets :**  They are digital sheets ,most common spreadsheets are Microsoft Excel and Google Sheets.
2. **Query Language :** Is a computer language that helps manipulate data from a database, some popular Structured Query Language (SQL) programs include MySQL, Microsoft SQL Server, and Big Query.
3. **Visualization Tool :** Data analysts use a number of visualization tools, like graphs, maps, tables, charts, and more. Two popular visualization tools are Tableau and Looker.
> [!NOTE] More Information refer : [[Key Data Analyst Tools]]

### Choose The Right Tool For The Job

- Depending on which phase of the data analysis process you’re in, you will need to use different tools.
- For creating complex and eye-catching visualizations, then the visualization tools Tableau and Looker are the best option
- For organizing, cleaning, and analyzing data, then you will probably be choosing between spreadsheets and databases using queries.
- Spreadsheets and databases both offer ways to store, manage, and use data. The basic content for both tools are sets of values. Yet, there are some key differences, too:

#### Comparison: Spreadsheets vs. Databases

|**Feature**|**Spreadsheets**|**Databases**|
|---|---|---|
|**Interface**|Accessed through a software application|Accessed using a query language|
|**Structure**|Organized in a row and column format|Organized using rules and relationships|
|**Architecture**|Organizes information in cells|Organizes information in complex collections|
|**Capacity**|Provides access to a limited amount of data|Provides access to huge amounts of data|
|**Data Entry**|Manual data entry|Strict and consistent data entry|
|**User Access**|Generally one user at a time|Multiple users|
|**Control**|Controlled by the user|Controlled by a database management system|
> [!INFO] Spreadsheets are suitable for organizing, cleaning, and analyzing small to medium datasets. Databases are ideal for storing, managing, and analyzing large and complex datasets.
