
# Module 1 : Ask Effective Questions
## Introduction

**Instructor:** Ximena (Google Finance Data Analyst)

**Core Focus: The "Ask" Phase** The "Ask" phase is the foundation of data analysis:

- **Purpose:** Define the business problem and align with stakeholder expectations.
    
- **Result:** Maintains focus on the specific problem, ensuring more successful outcomes.
    

### **Course Learning Roadmap:**

- **Questioning:** Mastering the difference between effective (insight-driven) and ineffective questions.
    
- **Data Types:** Understanding which data is most useful for specific situations.
    
- **Spreadsheets:** Advanced exploration to improve analysis efficiency.
    
- **Structured Thinking:** Breaking vague, complex problems into small, logical steps by:
    
    1. Recognizing the situation.
        
    2. Organizing available information.
        
    3. Identifying gaps and opportunities.
        
    4. Determining potential options.
        
- **Communication:** Proven strategies for sharing findings effectively with others.
    

### **Data Analysis Lifecycle Reminder:**

1. Ask → 2. Prepare → 3. Process → 4. Analyze → 5. Share → 6. Act

### Case Study: Anywhere Gaming Repair

**The Problem:** The owner of a mobile video game repair business wanted to expand but didn’t know which advertising strategy would be most effective and affordable for his target audience.

### Data Analysis Phases in Action

|**Phase**|**How it was applied to the case study**|
|---|---|
|**Ask**|Analyst Maria collaborated with stakeholders to define the core problem: identifying the best, most budget-friendly advertising method to reach the target audience.|
|**Prepare**|Maria collected data on video game system owners (demographics) and the costs/reach of various advertising methods (TV vs. podcasts).|
|**Process**|Maria cleaned the data to remove errors and inaccuracies, ensuring the final dataset was reliable and ready for analysis.|
|**Analyze**|Maria identified that the core target audience (ages 18–34) listens to podcasts. She recommended podcasts over TV due to budget constraints.|
|**Share**|Maria presented her findings to stakeholders using clear visuals, making it easy for them to understand the recommended solution.|
|**Act**|The business ran a 30-second podcast ad for one month, resulting in 85 new customers.|

_This case study perfectly illustrates how following the data lifecycle transforms a vague business goal into a measurable, successful outcome. When you're ready to start your own projects, remember this flow—it's the exact blueprint you'll use in a professional setting._
